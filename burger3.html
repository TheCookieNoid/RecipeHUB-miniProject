Greek Lamb Burger

Recipe by the mediterranean dish

Prep Time

20 mins

Cook Time

10 mins

Servings

8

Ingredients

Ground lamb 2 pounds (see note)

Small red onion 1, grated

Garlic cloves 2, minced

Fresh parsley 1 cup, chopped

Mint leaves 10, chopped

Dried oregano 2 1/2 teaspoons

Ground cumin 2 teaspoons

Paprika 1/2 teaspoon

Cayenne pepper 1/2 teaspoon (optional)

Kosher salt to taste

Black pepper to taste

Extra virgin olive oil as needed

To Serve:

Burger buns or pita bread as needed

Tzatziki sauce as needed

Sliced tomatoes as needed

Sliced green bell pepper as needed

Sliced cucumbers as needed

Sliced red onions as needed

Pitted Kalamata olives, sliced as needed

Crumbled feta as needed

Instructions

1. Make the lamb burger mixture. To a large mixing bowl, add the meat, onion, garlic, parsley, mint, oregano, cumin, paprika and cayenne. Season with a good pinch or two of kosher salt and pepper. Add a generous drizzle of extra virgin olive oil and mix until everything is well combined.

2. Make the lamb patties. Divide the meat mixture into 8 equal portions. Using both hands, work meat into smooth spheres, then lightly press to form into patties. Arrange the patties on a large tray or dish, and with the tip of your thumb, press lightly to form a shallow, somewhat wide, depression into the center of each patty (this will keep the patty flat as it cooks). Cover and refrigerate as you heat your grill.

3. Get ready to grill. Preheat an outdoor gas grill or an indoor griddle to medium. Get all your fixings ready to go.

4. Grill the lamb burgers. When your grill/griddle is hot, lightly oil and arrange the lamb patties on top. Cover and grill over medium heat for 3 to 5 minutes on each side. The internal temperature for a medium burger should register 160°F–adjust cooking time according to your desired doneness.

5. Rest and serve. Allow the lamb burgers to rest for 5 to 10 minutes so it stays juicy. Serve in buns or pita pockets with your fixings of choice.

Our goal is to offer the best recipes while minimizing waste. To avoid food waste if you don’t enjoy a recipe, we provide single-serving ingredient lists. Here’s the list:

For the Greek-Style Patty:

1. In a bowl, combine the ground lamb, grated red onion, minced garlic, chopped parsley, chopped mint, dried oregano, ground cumin, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper.

2. Mix until well combined. Form into a patty.

3. Heat a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.

4. Cook the patty for about 4-5 minutes on each side or until fully cooked.

To Serve:

1. Place the cooked patty on a burger bun or pita bread.

2. Top with tzatziki sauce, sliced tomato, green bell pepper, cucumber, red onion, Kalamata olives, and crumbled feta.
