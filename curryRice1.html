Coconut Curry Rice

Recipe by nospoonnecessary

Prep Time

10 mins

Cook Time

35 mins

Servings

4

Ingredients

Neutral Oil Canola, avocado, grapeseed, or vegetable oil all fit the bill. Ghee also works!

Scallions Also known as green onions. You’ll want both the white/pale greens to cook with, and then the pretty dark greens to add as garnish.

Garlic Fresh garlic can’t be beat, but you’re welcome to use minced jarred garlic or garlic paste for a shortcut.

Curry Powder This spice blend is warm without being spicy. If you’re feeling adventurous, try replacing it with another flavorful masala blend.

Grated Ginger Root Much like garlic, fresh is preferred. That said, feel free to use tube-style ginger paste or frozen ginger paste cubes.

Ground Coriander This bright, citrusy spice is actually the seed of the cilantro plant. If you only have whole coriander, even better — give it a quick toast in a dry pan until fragrant, then mash with a mortar & pestle or spice grinder.

Grated Carrots Grating carrots on a box grater is simple enough, but feel free to sub in pre-shredded carrots that you’ve roughly chopped.

Jasmine Rice This long-grain white rice has a subtle perfume that I can’t get enough of. Basmati rice is equally yummy!

Canned Coconut Milk Opt for full-fat canned coconut milk for the best flavor and texture. Be sure to mix it well before using.

Liquid of Choice You’ve got options, people! Chicken bouillon and water, veggie broth, chicken broth, or filtered H2O are all perfectly acceptable.

Cilantro IMHO, you can’t have Indian (or Thai or Mexican) food without a hefty sprinkling of fresh cilantro. If you’re one of the unfortunates who can only taste soap, swap in parsley leaves

Lime Optional, for garnish. A quick flurry of zest and squeeze of juice adds lovely brightness.

Instructions

1. Cook Aromatics: Heat the oil in a medium to large saucepan or heavy-bottomed, rimmed skillet over medium heat. Once the oil is shimmering, add the white and light green scallions, grated garlic, curry powder, grated ginger, and coriander. Season with ¾ teaspoon salt and a heaping ¼ teaspoon pepper. Cook, stirring constantly, for 1 minute, or until fragrant.

2. Add Carrots and Rice: Add the grated carrots and the long-grain rice to the pan. Cook, stirring constantly, until the rice is lightly toasted and fragrant, about 1-2 minutes.

3. Add Coconut Milk and Water: Pour the coconut milk and bullion water into the pan and give everything a good stir. Increase heat and bring the ingredients to a rapid simmer – without stirring.

4. Cover and Cook: Once simmering, immediately reduce the heat to low and cover the pan. Let the rice cook for 15-20 minutes, or until all the liquid is absorbed and the rice is tender. (Note: On a gas stove with a medium saucepan, my rice takes approximately 16 minutes.)

5. Let Stand Off Heat, Fluff Rice: Keep the rice covered and remove the pan from the heat. Let the rice steam, covered and off the heat, for 5 minutes. Then, remove the lid and use a fork to fluff the rice.

6. Add Remaining Herbs and Adjust Taste: Sprinkle the chopped cilantro leaves and dark green parts of the scallions all over the rice. Stir and then taste and adjust the curry carrot rice for seasoning.

7. Garnish and Serve: Garnish the curry with red pepper flakes and lime zest, if using. Serve warm with lime wedges on the side. Enjoy!

Our goal is to offer the best recipes while minimizing waste. To avoid food waste if you don’t enjoy a recipe, we provide single-serving ingredient lists. Here’s the list:

1/4 cup long-grain rice

1/4 cup coconut milk

1/4 cup water (with a small amount of bouillon dissolved in it)

1/4 medium carrot, grated

1/2 tablespoon oil

1/2 scallion (white and light green parts for cooking, dark green parts for garnish)

1/2 clove garlic, grated

1/4 teaspoon curry powder

1/4 teaspoon grated ginger

1/8 teaspoon ground coriander

Pinch of salt and pepper (adjust to taste)

1/2 tablespoon chopped cilantro (for garnish)

Optional garnishes: red pepper flakes, lime zest, lime wedge
