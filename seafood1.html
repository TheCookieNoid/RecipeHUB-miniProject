Baked Salmon

Recipe by tastes better from scratch

Prep Time

14 mins

Cook Time

15 mins

Servings

6

Ingredients

Salmon fillets 1 1/2-2 lbs

Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

For Garlic Butter Sauce:

Butter (melted) 3 tablespoons

Garlic (minced) 3 cloves

Low sodium chicken broth (or dry white wine) 1/4 cup

Lemon juice 2 tablespoons

Honey 1 teaspoon

Fresh chopped parsley and lemon slices (for garnish, optional)

For Cajun Seasoning:

Cajun seasoning to taste

Olive oil to taste

For Dill Seasoning:

Butter (melted) 1/2 cup

Freshly squeezed lemon juice 2 tablespoons

Lemon zest 2 teaspoons

Garlic (minced) 3 cloves

Dried dill weed (or fresh dill, chopped) 1 tablespoon (or 1/4 cup)

Nutrition

Instructions

1. Set salmon on counter and allow it to rest for 20-30 minutes to come to room temperature. Pat with a paper towel.

2. Lightly spray a sheet pan with non-stick cooking spray and place fillet on top, skin side down. Alternately, spray a large piece of aluminum foil with non-stick cooking spray and place fillet on top, skin side down, and then place on a baking tray.

3. Melt butter in a small saucepan over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté for 30 seconds. Add chicken broth and lemon juice. Cook for 3-5 more minutes to reduce the sauce. Stir in honey.

4. For Dill: Combine all ingredients in a small bowl.

5. For Cajun seasoning: spread olive oil over filet and sprinkle a thin, even layer of cajun seasoning all over the top of the fillet.

6. If cooking in foil, lift up all four sides of the tinfoil and seal together to create a pouch, to seal in the juices while the salmon bakes.

7. Bake at 400 degrees F for 12-15 minutes depending on thickness of salmon. For best results, test for doneness using a thermometer. Aim for no more than 140º F as the salmon will continue to cook after removing it from the oven. Or, use a fork to gently pull back on a section in the thickest part of the filet. The salmon should flake away easily and look opaque.
